02FaMVD Lisboa
verbum dei · Lisboa
Identity

A local family within the global Verbum Dei Family

The Missionary Family Verbum Dei of Lisbon is part of the Missionary Family Verbum Dei present across the five continents, at the service of the Word of God.

Belonging

People and groups from different states of life, ages, cultures and social conditions participate in the same spirituality and mission.

Local expression

Lisbon is formed through the presence of FMVD missionaries, missionary couples, committed missionary disciples and people who feel part of the FaMVD.

Common mission

To live and serve the charism in communion with the wider Verbum Dei Family and the Church.

Main challenges

What the organisational diagnosis identified

Coordination & knowledge

Limited knowledge of each area’s activity; opportunity for greater collaboration between groups.

Planning & communication

Planning is insufficiently documented and shared; communication between groups is informal and not sufficiently organised.

Decision-making

Need for clearer autonomy and responsibility in decisions.

Renewal of offering

Importance of varying the community’s offer and not only repeating what has always been done.

10FaMVD Lisboa

The next stage

From autonomy to co-responsibility

The next step is not to reduce initiative, but to make initiative more connected, sustainable and missionary.

Direction

A common direction that helps the community converge without losing the creativity of each group.

Participation

A broader sense of belonging, where more people feel invited to carry the community.

Support

Clearer accompaniment of groups, transition folders, shared planning and better internal communication.

11FaMVD Lisboa

Signs of the Way

What we feel called to deepen

1. Encounter Jesus in the Word

Return to the source of the charism: prayer, listening, biblical formation and personal encounter with Christ in the Word.

2. Welcome and create bonds

Strengthen belonging, fraternity, care and integration of those who arrive, especially in contexts of fragility.

3. Deepen missionary dynamism

Read the signs of the times, understand what people are seeking, and offer accessible paths of encounter, community and mission.
